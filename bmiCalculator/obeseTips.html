Obese

Your results for this BMI test have concluded that your weight is considered “obese” (BMI over 30). This puts you at risk for high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, heart disease, stroke, cancer, sleep problems, and much more. But that is ok! People can control their weight and can become a better version of themselves. The best way to reduce your weight is to monitor your calorie intake, manage your stress levels, sleep well, and incorporating an active lifestyle in your day-to-day routine. Check out our Calorie Calculator and Calories Burned Calculator to see your calorie intake and how many calories you burn doing any of your favorite activities. You should also be aware of what foods you are eating. Only have sugary drinks such as soda, fast food, processed foods such as chips, candy, cake, alcohol, white bread, and any food with a lot of added sugars or added fats occasionally. Make sure to not get too stressed while losing weight, as that can slow down your metabolism. A nice trick is to decrease your plate size, as this makes your brain think that you are eating more food, even though you are eating less. On top of this, make sure to get adequate sleep. Check out the CDC recommendations for sleep.
(Note that BMI tests are not always accurate as the calculations do not take in muscle mass).
