Healthy

Your results for this Body Fat Percentage test have concluded that your body fat condition is considered “healthy” (From 6-25 percent). This reduces your risk for high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, heart disease, stroke, cancer, sleep problems, and much more. Your body fat percentage still can grow, but you can prevent this by monitoring your calorie intake, managing your stress levels, sleeping well, and incorporating an active lifestyle in your day-to-day routine. Check out our Calorie Calculator and Calories Burned Calculator to see your calorie intake and how many calories you burn doing any of your favorite activities. Try not to have sugary drinks such as soda, fast food, processed foods such as chips, candy, cake, alcohol, white bread, and any food with a lot of added sugars or added fats as an everyday item. Make sure to not get too stressed in your day-to-day life, as that can result in sudden body fat percentage gain. On top of this, make sure to get adequate sleep. Check out the CDC recommendations for sleep.
(Note that BMI tests are not always accurate as the calculations do not take in muscle mass).
