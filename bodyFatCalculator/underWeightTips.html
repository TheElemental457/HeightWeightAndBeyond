Underweight

Your results for this BMI test have concluded that your weight is considered “underweight” (BMI under 18.5). This increases your chances for malnutrition, development delays, and much more. To gain weight, make sure to eat 3 meals a day, exercise to gain muscle fat, increase your protein and carb intake, and increase your intake of energy-dense foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes. Try not to gain weight on foods such as soda, cake, chips, or anything classified as “junk food.” This may give you an initial weight boost, but this can increase your chances of heart disease, cancer, diabetes, and more. Make sure to eat all 3 meals of the day and eat as many calories as recommended in our Calorie Calculator. To gain muscle weight, eat protein heavy foods such as eggs, dairy, meats such as chicken, turkey, and fish. Do any of your favorite exercises to gain muscle mass. On top of this, make sure to get adequate sleep. Check out the CDC recommendations for sleep.
(Note that BMI tests are not always accurate as the calculations do not take in muscle mass).
