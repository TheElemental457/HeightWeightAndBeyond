Turning Wheels Program and their goals

"TurningWheels for Kids is a program of the Valley Medical Center Foundation, a 501c3 nonprofit organization (Federal Tax ID 77-0187890), that distributes bikes not only at the holiday but also year round through a number of partnerships focused on preventing childhood obesity and supporting low-income families. TurningWheels For Kids work with numerous partners, including the Pediatric Healthy Lifestyle Center at Santa Clara Valley Medical Center, to encourage outdoor activity—like riding a bike to school or to hang out with friends—as a way to improve children’s health and well-being. “We must recognize that the epidemic of childhood obesity is connected to sedentary pastimes: TVs, computers, texting, and video games,” says Sue. “We must hit a reset button and bring children back outdoors to experience the fun we all remember from our childhoods. We must give children the opportunity to share bike riding as an important childhood rite of passage. And as the number one gift requested by these children, bikes can bring kids back outdoors to active play—exactly what a child should be doing.” - Turning Wheels Website
