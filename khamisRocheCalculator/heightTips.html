Gaining Height

Most of a person's height is determined by genetics (60-80%), and the body grows by lengthening its long bones (thanks to special cartilage located in long bones called growth plates). It is impossible to increase bone length after the closure of growth plates. This means that you cannot gain height after puberty. During the childhood and teenage years, while puberty is on, a healthy lifestyle allows a child to grow in the right way. At that time, it is especially important to sleep enough, eat healthy food, and have good posture. Check out the CDC recommendations for how much sleep you should get from a day-to-day basis. A balanced diet must include proteins, carbohydrates, fat, and vitamins in the correct proportion to get good enough nutrients for the child to grow to the expected height.
