How to use the calculator

Simply click on the measuring system you use (imperial or metric), and enter your weight, height, age, mother’s height, and father’s height. You can now see your future height estimates and how to increase your height as a child!

How the calculator works

This calculator uses data tables made by Dr. Alex Roche and Henry Khamis to predict childhood height for people from ages 4-17.5. It takes in your weight, height, gender, mom’s height, and dad’s height. If you are using the metric system, behind the scenes it converts weight and height into imperial. The data tables are found using the gender and age. It gets an array of coefficients from table used with the following formula: (coefficient [0] * weight) + (coefficient [1] * height) + (coefficient [2] * ((mom’s height/2) + (dad’s height/2))) + (coefficient [3]). (Note that this calculator works best on Caucasian origin children and has a margin of around 1.5 inches or 3.8 centimeters).
